Expert Advice

CV Writing Tips

Follow these tips from our recruitment experts to create a CV that gets noticed

01

Keep It Concise

Aim for 2–3 pages maximum. Recruiters scan CVs quickly — make every word count and focus on your most relevant experience.

02

Tailor for Each Role

Customise your CV for each application. Highlight skills and achievements that match the specific job requirements.

03

Quantify Achievements

Use numbers and percentages to demonstrate impact. "Increased sales by 30%" is far more compelling than "improved sales".

04

Professional Format

Use a clean, consistent layout with clear headings. Avoid excessive graphics that can confuse applicant tracking systems.

05

Include Keywords

Mirror the language used in the job description. Many companies use automated screening before a human sees your CV.

06

Proofread Carefully

Spelling and grammar errors can cost you the interview. Have someone else review your CV before submitting.

What To Include

Essential CV Sections

Make sure your CV includes these key sections to give recruiters everything they need

1

Personal Details

Full name, contact number, email address, location, and LinkedIn profile URL.

2

Professional Summary

A 3–4 line overview of your experience, skills, and what you bring to the table.

3

Work Experience

List roles in reverse chronological order with company, job title, dates, and key achievements.

4

Education

Qualifications, institution names, and completion dates. Include relevant certifications.

5

Key Skills

A concise list of technical and soft skills relevant to the role you're applying for.

6

References

Include 2–3 professional references, or note "Available upon request" if preferred.
